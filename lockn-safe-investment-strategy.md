## 1. SAFE Structure Recommendation

### Post-Money vs Pre-Money: Use Post-Money SAFEs

**Recommendation: Post-money SAFE (YC standard template)**

- **Industry standard** ‚Äî 92% of pre-seed rounds use SAFEs (Carta Q3 2025), and post-money with valuation cap only is the dominant structure
- **Clarity** ‚Äî Post-money SAFEs give you exact dilution visibility. You know precisely what % each investor gets. Pre-money SAFEs create ambiguity because dilution shifts as you raise more
- **Investor familiarity** ‚Äî Angels and institutional investors expect YC post-money SAFEs. Deviating creates friction and signals inexperience
- **Use the standard YC SAFE docs** from ycombinator.com/documents ‚Äî don't modify them

### Valuation Cap Range

Based on current market data (Carta 2025):

| Round Size | Median Post-Money Cap (2025) | LockN Recommended Range |
|---|---|---|
| Under $250K | $7.5M | **$5M‚Äì$8M** |
| $250K‚Äì$500K | $10M‚Äì$12M | **$8M‚Äì$10M** |
| $500K+ | $15M‚Äì$20M | **$10M‚Äì$12M** |

**For LockN's initial raise, target a $6M‚Äì$8M post-money cap.**

Rationale:
- Pre-revenue proptech/IoT typically commands lower caps than SaaS or AI ($15M‚Äì$35M median)
- Hardware-adjacent startups face a "show me" discount vs pure software
- A $6M‚Äì$8M cap is attractive to angels (meaningful upside) while not undervaluing the business
- Leaves room to raise a subsequent SAFE at $10M‚Äì$15M after hitting revenue milestones

### Discount Rate

**Recommendation: No discount. Cap only.**

- The dominant market standard is cap-only (no discount)
- Adding a discount complicates the instrument and signals desperation
- If an investor insists on a discount, offer 15‚Äì20% max as a concession
- Never stack cap + discount ‚Äî it creates a "double dip"

### Pro-Rata Rights

**Recommendation: Offer pro-rata side letter only to investors writing $25K+**

- Standard YC SAFEs don't include pro-rata by default
- Offering it selectively to larger checks incentivizes bigger investments
- Keep the SAFE clean; use a separate side letter for pro-rata
- This is a meaningful sweetener that costs you nothing today

## 4. Legal & Compliance Checklist

### Must-Have Before Issuing SAFEs

- [ ] **Delaware C-Corp** ‚Äî If not already incorporated, form a Delaware C-Corp (standard for VC-backed startups). Cost: ~$500‚Äì$1,500 with a service like Stripe Atlas, Clerky, or Firstbase
- [ ] **Board resolution** authorizing SAFE issuance
- [ ] **83(b) election** filed (if founder equity was recently issued ‚Äî must be within 30 days of grant)
- [ ] **Cap table management** ‚Äî Set up on Carta, Pulley, or AngelList Stack (free tier available)

### Securities Law Compliance

- [ ] **Regulation D, Rule 506(b)** ‚Äî Most common exemption for SAFE rounds
  - Unlimited raise amount
  - No general solicitation (no public advertising)
  - Up to 35 non-accredited investors (but ideally stick to accredited only)
  - Self-certification questionnaire is sufficient for accredited verification under 506(b)
- [ ] **Form D filing** with SEC ‚Äî Required within 15 days of first sale. File electronically on EDGAR. Cost: $0
- [ ] **State blue sky filings** ‚Äî Most states require notice filing after Form D. Varies by state; typically $50‚Äì$300 per state where investors reside
- [ ] **Accredited investor verification** ‚Äî For 506(b): investor self-certification via questionnaire. For 506(c) (general solicitation): must use third-party verification (tax returns, broker letter, etc.)

### Documents Needed Per Investor

- [ ] Post-money SAFE agreement (YC template)
- [ ] Pro-rata side letter (if applicable)
- [ ] Accredited investor questionnaire
- [ ] Investor information form (name, address, SSN/EIN for tax reporting)

### Recommended Legal Support

- **Budget option:** Clerky ($500‚Äì$2,000 for full SAFE package including Form D)
- **Standard:** Startup-focused attorney ($3,000‚Äì$7,000 for full round)
- **Top-tier:** Cooley, Goodwin, Gunderson ‚Äî typically offer deferred billing for funded startups
- **Do NOT skip legal counsel.** A botched securities offering creates liability that can torpedo a Series A.
